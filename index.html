Abstract

Medical fibers are the monomers 1 of biotextiles that could be either natural polymers or synthetic (manmade ones). The difference between synthetic and natural polymers is that synthetic ones can be synthesized and modified in a controlled manner to achieve a specific properties for a given application.1 Both have shown to be effective as suitable suture materials especially natural polymers such as silk and linen fibers which have been used for over 500 years and non-absorbable polymers such as nylon, polypropylene and other monofilament sutures with absorbable properties.
